Overview

Computer Science Seniors (CSS) was a team-oriented project at UCSD that focused on UX and UI design. Originally a ten week course, we were given just five weeks to conceptualize, prototype, revise and develop an innovative application. Our application, Homii, is a housing search engine for students that combines elements of real estate and social media to find the perfect living space and roommates for you.



Conceptualization

Our team consists of six computer science seniors from UCSD who have all experienced the difficulty of finding affordable housing in La Jolla, which had the highest home prices in the nation in 2008 and 2009. At the time, two of the team members were in the exact situation while looking for housing for the upcoming Fall quarter. Many housing search engines existed, but they were divided into two categories: (1) sites such as Zillow that do a great job of finding housing, but fail to help you find roommates or (2) sites like the Facebook UCSD Housing Group that does a great job of finding roommates, but fails to help you find real estate. We decided that this was an issue that was most pertinent to the class as it involves many students at UCSD. Thus, Homii was established.
Other contenders included

  1. Social Scanner - An app that searches the surrounding area within a given radius for events and people nearby.
  2. Gym Goer - An app or website that lets you know how many people are in the gym at any given time so you can workout when it's least crowded
  3. Study Buddy - Students pin their location on a campus map as well as what theyre studying so that others around them can join them.
